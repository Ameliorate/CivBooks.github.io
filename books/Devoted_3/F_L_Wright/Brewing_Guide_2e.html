Cauldron Make a cauldron from 7 iron ingots. Place it over an empty block and light that with flint and steel. A bucket of water fills it and 3 bottles empty it. For research use 9.
Page 7 of 38
Brewing Stand A Brewing Stand with Glowstone Dust in the top right compartment becomes a still without consuming the glowstone. The Blaze Rod can be hard to source but can be traded after this Christmas provided lots.
Page 8 of 38
Barrels Oak, Spruce, Birch, Acacia, Jungle, and Dark Oak wood make different barrels. To construct a small one use 8 stairs and a sign with BARREL in the first line on the top right front corner of the barrel. ...
Page 9 of 38
... A large barrel is made from 16 stairs, 18 planks, 5 fences, and the sign in the center top front. One fence is the tap. Add sign last. Console confirms creation. Small barrels better economize space.
Page 10 of 38
Bottles Sand in a furnace makes glass, and 3 glass make 3 bottles. You will need more. Use a smelter factory. Bottles are also good for storing XP at an enchant table before getting too drunk.
Page 11 of 38
Bucket Brewers only need one, but they need it all the time... ...to fill cauldrons ...to get milk antidote ...to put out fires after the nation had another Rum party... It can also make water slides down cliffs.
Page 12 of 38
Water Source Put it near the cauldrons. Water replicates above solid blocks to fill 1 block in between or around a corner. So a 2x2 well provides forever. Build it so you can't fall in.
Page 13 of 38
Clock Made from 4 Gold Ingots around a Redstone Dust, this usually frivolous tools is essential for brewing. Touch it to a cauldron and the current fermentation time is shown.
Page 14 of 38
Cow A cow is a good friend to have during long hours in the brewery. Its gentle mooing will become your mantra. And enough milk cures any game effects like poisoning, nausea, blindness, slurred chat...
Page 15 of 38
Timer Some brews take a long time. Most require precise timing. Have an alarm ready to set, or you will find Ruined Potions aged 34 years in your barrels. Time flies like an arrow. Fruit flies like a banana.

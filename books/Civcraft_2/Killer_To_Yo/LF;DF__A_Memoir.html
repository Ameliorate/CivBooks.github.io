§lLive Free or Die Frosty§r ------------------- Knew a guy named Tom. Big ideas guy. Me and Tom were at a bar one night, just shooting the shit. Tom was an interesting guy, I just liked hearing what he thought,
Page 2 of 23
I didn't have to say much. Usually Tom's ideas were flimsy. He just threw them at me like a ball to a wall, trying to see what I might bounce back. Well that night he actually had a good idea, so for once I actually agreed with him.

"You know what people like? Bars." he said. I was interested in seeing where this matter-of-fact line of thinking was going. He continued "You know what else people like? Ice cream." Now for a moment he almost lost me, but then I got to thinking.
Page 4 of 23
There ain't no food I wanted more when I was sippin' scotch than some Neapolitan ice cream. I told him as much, I said "Tom, that might be the best idea you've ever had." Tom just lit up at that. I always thought his ideas were
Page 5 of 23
interesting (to say the least), but I never told them they were good. Things moved pretty fast from there. Tom and I both were saving up to invest in something and this seemed like the perfect time. I bought a bar, and together we moved in a big
Page 6 of 23
ice cream freezer. And then we were open for business. Me with my bar, and Tom with his ice cream. At first it was a pretty 50/50 split with people buying about as much ice cream as they were booze, but that was when Tom started gettin' money-eyes.
Page 7 of 23
He started upping the price of his scoops which meant less money being spent on liquor and more being spent on ice cream. At first it didn't bother me as much, so what if he made a fraction more than me. But then I guess
Page 8 of 23
just one freezer wasn't enough for Tom. He bought the business next door and renovated it into a full on ice cream parlour. Now people were getting just a little tipsy at my bar only to head over to your shop and stay there for the rest of the night.
Page 9 of 23
It was becoming more than just a little issue. Soon Tom started selling more than just scoops, though. Rum and coke floats, tequila-vanilla milkshakes, a whole line of alcoholic beverages that cut me right out of the process.
Page 10 of 23
By then there was nothin' I could do. Sure I could offer the old favourites but by then Tom was working completely autonomously from me. Any why wouldn't he? Mixing booze with the ice cream solves the trouble of having me around. It solves it just fine.
Page 11 of 23
It was about that time I started dipping in to my own supply. I neglected my owners' duties and my so-called half, now more of a quarter or even tenth, of the business fell into disrepair. It wasn't long before we brought lawyers into this and there
Page 12 of 23
was more than just a brick wall dividing our businesses, our two former halves. With my investment lost and the bills piling up, I pawned everything not nailed down. You see, I know a guy. Nick, from uptown. He got into a rough crowd, so I ask if he
Page 13 of 23
knows a guy who can take care of my problem for me. Nearly bankrupt, I spend every last nickel to put down a hit on Tom. It was him I wanted now, whether I made it through or not. Poor Tom had no chance. He was picked off easy with a
Page 14 of 23
clean shot to the head on his way to wherever it was he was going. With my old partner dead, I got full control over both the bar and the ice cream parlour. Or at least that's what I thought'd happen.

problem now that his task was done. Told me he'd do me in for free if I came beggin' again. I don't know how the kid found out, but he did. He must've pieced it together somehow. He kicked down the door of my bar's boarded doors. I only saw his

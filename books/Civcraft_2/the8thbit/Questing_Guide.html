Welcome to the Quest Board, comrade! Whether you're an aspiring citizen of Trenzalore or an old fogey who can't remember the rules because you have some sort of exotic old person brain disorder, you've come to the right place!
Page 2 of 28
The Quest Board is where citizens of Trenzalore come to pay tribute. What is paying tribute?
Page 3 of 28
If you help out by building in town, defending the town, mining, or really doing anything helpful you're already doing so to an extent. The Quest Board is just a way to track those contributions.
Page 4 of 28
Everyone who pays tribute of at least 200 'points' (you get points by completing the aforementioned tasks) becomes a citizen of Trenzalore. Citizens get to:
Page 5 of 28
* build houses behind the town wall * add people to the guest group * access the citizen storage (free stuff!) * run for council * start a shop in the bazaar
Page 6 of 28
Don't want to pay tribute? That's perfectly fine. If you decide not to you will stay in the guest group. Guests are welcome to build outside of the town wall.
Page 7 of 28
So how exactly do these quests work? Every guest must gain 200 points (200p) in order to become a citizen, and every citizen must gain 100 points (100p) per week to maintain their citizenship.
Page 8 of 28
To gain points, go down the aisles in the Quest Board floor and find a quest (listed on the signs above chests) which interestes you.
Page 9 of 28
Each quest sign will include the name of the quest, the amount of points the quest is worth, and the number of times the quest can be completed within a week in brackets.
Page 10 of 28
If there is an X in the brackets it can be done infinite times. Example signs follow:
Page 11 of 28
Mine 1d 10p [X] Harvest Potatoes 5p [28] Finish Gravel Road Around Wall 15p [1]
Page 12 of 28
When you find a quest you like, open the chest below its sign. You'll find a single book with a short description of the quest.
Page 13 of 28
If there aren't already as many names as the number in brackets below the description, add your name and the date below like so:
Page 14 of 28
the8thbit (05/30/2013-
Page 15 of 28
Then go complete the quest. If your quest was to gather resources, put those resources in the hoppers in the main room. Once you complete it, come back and put the date of completion, like so:
Page 16 of 28
the8thbit (05/30/2013-05/31/2013)
Page 17 of 28
Congratulations, you just got some points! You can use the other book in this chest to manage your total score for the week.
Page 18 of 28
As stated, you must get 100p per week in order to maintain your citizenship. If you fail to do this two weeks in a row, you will lose it and all of your benefits. You will then need to gain 200p in order to join the citizen group again.
Page 19 of 28
On the Quest Board there are 5 labeled aisles: Resources, Land, Foreign Affairs, Other, and Community. The first four catagories are normal quests placed by council members. The Community Quests, however, are special.
Page 20 of 28
Community Quests can by placed by *any* citizen. These postings are worth 5p each, and are always treated like they have a [1]. If the quest involves gathering resources, you give the resources to the other player instead of the hoppers in the main room.
Page 21 of 28
Because the point value and repeatability is contstant, instead of having those listed, the person who posted the quest will put their name on the second line. Example Community Quest:
Page 22 of 28
Mine 16 quarts the8thbit
Page 23 of 28
Once you complete a Community Quest, please destroy its sign. You can then, if you'd like, put your own quest in its place.
Page 24 of 28
If you ever have a situation where you are not able to pay tribute a week (say you go on vacation, or you are very busy with other things that week) you can contact a council member, and we can consider and exception.
Page 25 of 28
And on the other end of things, feel free to exceed your tribute goal (200p/100p). We can use all of the mutual aid we can get, and any extra will be greatly apreciated.
Page 26 of 28

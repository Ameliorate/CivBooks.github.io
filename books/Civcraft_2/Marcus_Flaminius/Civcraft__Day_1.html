I spawn in the midst of lonely madness. The sun is high in the sky, illuminating everything around me. I stand in a grass covered field, but seemingly random pieces of hoed dirt are scattered all around. They seem to almost be making a path. Perhaps
Page 3 of 16
it will lead me to some where safe and full of life. Perhaps it's the only thing for thousands of blocks. Either way, I see nothing else to do, so I follow the path. I travel perhaps 100 blocks before the path evolved into cobblestone markers,

each separated from one another by 10 or so blocks. Each has a torch on top. I take a few, fearing I won't have any more light later should I miss this opportunity. I realize that I haven't seen a tree so far on my journey. They have been replaced by

seem to be dirt pillars leading to nothing above. Some touch the sky while others I can reach. The path ends at an ocean. I can see no more land no matter how far I look. I turn back to venture off into the grassland, more lonely inside then

ever before. Where is everyone? Do they hide from me? I come across a bridge with dozens of pillars reaching to ascend it. I climb a set of dirt stairs, likely made by a lost traveler much like myself. Everything is still quiet, but I feel
Page 7 of 16
like I'm coming closer to life. Perhaps I will find people by the end of the night. I choose a direction of the bridge and travel. Cobblestone ruins appear just to the left of the bridge, but they look as though they were abandoned long ago. Still, I

have hope that I'll find more. Someone needed to have mined that cobblestone with a pickaxe, which means there must be wood eventually. I walk back from the ruins to a river which had been snaking under the bridge for some time. On it I see

two boats. I'm not alone, I think to myself. Overcome with both fear and excitement at the same time. I call out hoping for a reply, but receive nothing. I feel guilty as I do it, but I take the boat and begin following the bridge from below. I've

grown hungry and do not have the energy to run on the bridge now, so this will be how I travel. The sun begins to set. Not much longer the river begins to move away from the bridge, and I leave my boat to travel on it once more. Oh how long this

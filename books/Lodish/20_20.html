20/20 by Linda Brewer By the time they reached Indiana, Bill realized that Ruthie, his driving companion, was incapable of theoretical debate. She drove okay, she went halves on gas, etc., but she refused

argue. She didn't seem to know how. Bill was used to East Coast women who disputed everything he said, every step of the way. Ruthie stuck to simple observation, like "look, cows." He choked it up to the fact she was from rural Ohio and thrilled to death

to be anywhere else. She didn't mind driving into the setting sun. the third evening out, Bill rested his eyes while she cruised along making the occasional announcement. "Indian paintbrush. A golden eagle."

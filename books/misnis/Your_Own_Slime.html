Chapter 2: Making a good cage ~~~ A good cage for a slime emulates one of the natural habitats it has. The floor should be either moss, grass or solid material. If you choose to make the floor out of moss or grass, make sure that the slime has an
Page 10 of 30
adequate source of water to bathe themselves in. Note that slimes have a hard time navigating water, so you will need to provide a slab stepping stone to help them out of the water, and the pool shouldn't be more than 1 meter deep. An alternate way is to
Page 11 of 30
drizzle water on the slime or let water drip through the ceiling. If you choose to make the floor of solid material, it should be completely solid with no cracks or edges in it, and preferably not very conductive of heat. Solid stone works well for this
Page 12 of 30
purpose. If your floor has cracks in it, like cobblestone, your slime's health and size will degenerate as it loses slime down the cracks. You can use as a gentler alternative to chopping off parts of your slime, if you wish to reduce its size. Make sure
Page 13 of 30
the slime has plenty of water to help it recover health if so. Slimes' eyes are relatively poor. They cannot perceive glass or ice, unless it's stained or colored. Even if your slime is tame, it will make attempts at getting through clear materials.

fine, however. Lastly, the dimensions of your cage. it is HIGHLY recommended that you make the cage big enough to let the slime jump as high as they can. Maintaining the health of a slime that can't fully jump is very difficult, and should only be

Chapter 3: Your slime's childhood ~~~ When you have your cage ready to go, place the slime eggs in there and smother them with slime ooze collected from other slimes. If you do not have any of this, don't worry.
Page 18 of 30
The eggs will not hatch as long as you don't have the goop, so if they're near water and in a secured cage, you can journey to get the ooze you need with no worries. Once you have them smothered, they may either hatch right away or continue incubating
Page 19 of 30
if they didn't finish as you built the cage. If they're still incubating, keep smothering the eggs in ooze daily and they should hatch shortly. Contrary to popular belief, the small slimes usually seen in habitats are NOT baby slimes. In fact, tiny and
Page 20 of 30
small slimes are more likely to have lived longer than larger ones. Do not be scared when you see your pet slime for the first time - until slimes reach adulthood, they have no face or visible front side. They are completely blind and have a hard time
Page 21 of 30
remembering things, so make sure you are very careful with them. Slimes grow to size within 24 hours, and the childhood will usually last only a month with a good environment. You should feed your slime mostly soft material - plants like cacti or regular
Page 22 of 30
human food work well for this. Just put the food in the enclosure and the slime will eat whenever it needs to. A slime's childhood is the very best time to bond with it. Getting a lot of physical contact with the slime at this point will teach it that
Page 23 of 30
you aren't food, and it won't hurt a lot since baby slimes aren't very adept at predating. However, since your slime can neither see nor smell nor remember very well, it'll be hard for them to recognize you once they reach adulthood.

A good method for helping your slime recognize you is to whistle the same short melody every time you bond with your slime as a baby. This tune will help them recognize you without getting too close once your slime is an adult.

Chapter 4: Raising your slime ~~~ Once your slime reaches adulthood, give yourself a pat on the back. Adult slimes are largely self-sufficient and will require little additional training. Adult slimes are excellent
Page 26 of 30
if not unusual combat partners, as other monsters will not react with hostility until the slime has begun attacking. Slimes are the only overworld monster that have been successfully tamed so far.

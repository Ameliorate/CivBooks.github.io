II. What is Libertarian Socialism? In short, Libertarian Socialism is the abolition of hierarchically arranged, authoritarian institutions that control the means of production, AKA both the State and Capitalism. It is an
Page 8 of 29
umbrella term that spans Mutualism, Anarcho-Communism, and Anarcho-Syndicalism, all of which are accepted modes for communes within LSIF's ranks.

How do LSIF communes demonstrate Libertarian Socialism? The basic unit of the LSIF is the commune, all of which are voluntarily formed of free-associating individuals, politically and economically united for common
Page 11 of 29
prosperity. City-based, democratic management of surpluses, city planning, and international relations. Large-scale alterations to communes, large resouce allocations, and international moves
Page 12 of 29
moves are typically handled by the democratic assemblies, as far member approvals/expulsions and some legal matters. Common ownership of productive tools, structures, foods, armaments. Property constructed within

commune borders remain communal even after its original builders leave the Federation or the server. Both central and decentral storage for working materials and valuables, as appropriate, to provide a balance between ease of access and security.

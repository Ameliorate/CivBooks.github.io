I joined the server after seeing an advert for the Pumpkin Jacks somewhere. This started me off on my Civcraft career.
Page 4 of 47
I helped with the excavation and construction of Jack's Hold, meeting the original members Noxy, Jackalope, RKWildcard and KineticWaffle (.probably a few others as well but I don't remember them..)
Page 5 of 47
Jack's Hold was awesome, a huge IRO fortress in the centre, strong high walls and embassy towers all around. I first found out about Mt Augusta via their embassy tower.
Page 6 of 47
I played with the Jacks for a while. One of my clearest memories is myself, Noxy and Wildcard protted up on a roof overlooking Kizantium's main square, trying to pacify a possible riot.
Page 7 of 47
Back then, the Romans in Kizantium (.including r3kon.) had an ongoing protest with Columbia, the largest and most prosperous city on 1.0.
Page 8 of 47
I also recall first talking to Sami - now Saleel - about the way I was treating rogersd, the founder and sole member of the 'grefer hunters'. I apologised as I had not realised he was a young child.
Page 9 of 47
After a while, the Jacks became slightly inactive and subreddit opinion swung against us, painting us as warlike barbarians. Our belief in a strong state put us at natural loggerheads with the then mainly anarcho-capitalist population of the server.
Page 10 of 47
I logged in one day to find griefed buildings and no one else I knew online, so I decided to leave and see what else the server held. I entered the nether and made my way to Columbia, which had recently fallen.
Page 11 of 47
What had been a huge, active metropolis was now pretty much a ghost town, giant empty skyscrapers towering over silent streets.
Page 12 of 47
I found a plot and began to build a small church (.they're one of the few buildings I can design to a high standard, for some reason.) and put my tiny amount of wealth in a dropchest in the desert nearby.
Page 13 of 47
As I was building the church, one of the few still active Columbians came along and warned me that even though no one currently owned the land,
Page 14 of 47
if one of the previous owners logged back in he would be within his rights to pearl me and destroy what I had built. This pretty much ended my short stay in Columbia.
Page 15 of 47
While there, I had seen and explored a huge, abandoned commune called Goldmayne in the then traditional LSIF Hive-Tower style. I was intrigued but also tired of dealing with restrictive laws and inactive friends.
Page 16 of 47
I was pretty burnt out at this time so I left for a few weeks.
Page 17 of 47
On returning, I decided to hermit for a while. I found and claimed an island and began to construct an ironic roleplay faction in homage to the DDR (.hence my account name Erich_Honecker_..)
Page 18 of 47
One day while traveling the nether roads I found a copy of People's News Today by ZombieLenin, and realised the server didn't need two zombified ex-Soviet dictators.
Page 19 of 47
I stayed on my island for a while, doing typical hermit things before I got bored of the isolation and poverty inherent in such a lifestyle.
Page 20 of 47
Near to my island was the city of Mt Augusta - one of the oldest continuously populated cities on the server, along with Aristopolis, Panneton and Haven, and also one of the cities most open to new citizens.
Page 21 of 47
I was given a plot and built a small house, and began to immerse myself in the city and its people. In Mount Augusta was the then most active LSIF commune, Ataraxia.
Page 22 of 47
Intrigued by their ideology (.Libertarian Socialism or Anarcho-Communism.) I asked to join, and after a short trial period and the symbolic offering of charcoal produced at the commune, I was given membership.
Page 23 of 47
This was the beginning of the best time I had on 1.0. I spent the vast majority of my 1.0 playtime as a member of the LSIF.
Page 24 of 47
It would take far too long to write out everything I did with them, but the short version is that they were great friends and we built cities, produced surpluses and spread our ideology as far as we could.
Page 25 of 47
We had nether communes, secret hideaways and of course Panneton, the home commune. It really has to be seen to be believed - a huge beehive incorporating all kinds of farms, mob grinders, libraries, and so on.
Page 26 of 47
While I was a part of the LSIF, I also spent a lot of time in the CKC (.Cool Kids Club.) channel on mumble. This was where a lot of the ancaps hung out, but plenty of others were also there, from all across the political spectrum.
Page 27 of 47

Page 28 of 47
he once called me his 'favourite East German communist'. Back then, the server community was smaller and more inter- connected - You could travel to the other side of the map in 30 minutes and most everyone knew each other on some level.

At some point we in the LSIF started to get paranoid about ancap attacks on us and we built several hidden bases, like Red Exodus. At around this time, the first HCF invasion occurred.
Page 30 of 47

Trading is a skill Trading is a skill. It takes time to build up knowledge of where all the shops are, what people sell and don't sell, and what a fair price is.
Page 3 of 25
It takes time and repeated interaction to build up relationships with suppliers and consumers of different goods. It takes experience to design a shop that people actually like to use.
Page 4 of 25
All of this is fun for many people. It may not be for everyone, just like how not everyone likes building, farming, politicking, or PvPing. If you suck at one of these, you build relationships with people who are good at them.
Page 5 of 25
The point of the server is to be political; if you can survive without interacting with other players, the server is failing.
Page 6 of 25
Trading makes the community better Trade is also a basis of trust. In an online game where relationships are fleeting and people tend to fuck with each other because they're anonymous,
Page 7 of 25
trading can build trust and start a positive relationship, which can eventually become a friendship. That's good inherently, and also important for building tight-knit, non-toxic communities.
Page 8 of 25
Scarcity drives conflict When two people both want a resource but only one can have it, you have conflict. The two can resolve it diplomatically or through force. Either way, that's fun and interesting.
Page 9 of 25
Without scarcity, there is no reason for players to fight each other except personal grudges or misunderstandings between them, neither of which lets players exercise their skills for 4D chess.
Page 10 of 25
And, assuming this server was full of reasonable people (which it obviously isn't), then there would be no conflict at all and it'd basically be a survival server with some plugins to make it easier to survive.
Page 11 of 25
If you enjoy PvP or politics, then you are for scarcity.
Page 12 of 25
Building material scarcity makes the world more fun to explore One argument I hear for making build materials more easy to get is that building is fun for a lot of players,
Page 13 of 25
and having access to every material makes it more fun for them. While that may be true, it is antithetical to the points I argued above. I would bet that building with constraints is fun for a lot of people too.
Page 14 of 25
Further, it actually makes the world less fun to explore. If every city has access to the same building blocks at the same costs, then every city will look mostly the same.
Page 15 of 25
If cities only have access to a few building blocks for cheap and others are more difficult to obtain without trading, then builders will have to creatively use what is available to them.
Page 16 of 25
And that mean cities will look vastly different based on where they are located, and that's fun to explore. (We see this in the real world as well; why would you travel if every city looked like Birmingham?
Page 17 of 25
Why are some cities more popular tourist destinations than others? Usually it's because it's interesting to see how different cities have adapted to use what was available to them, food-wise, building-wise and manner-wise.)
Page 18 of 25
I would be more sympathetic if we were using an old version of minecraft with only oak wood and stone, but even if you're stuck in one biome there is still a lot of creative building you can do.
Page 19 of 25
What to do if you need something Obviously, not having access to things is a little annoying. Here's how you can get the resources you need:
Page 20 of 25
• Gather it yourself • Join the exchange Discord (ask 597 for a link) and ask for help • Use the exchange subreddit or even /r/civclassics
Page 21 of 25
• Use the trade/market channels that each city has • Ask for advice from someone who spends a lot of their time trading People will supply you if you are willing to pay.
Page 22 of 25
Conclusion Nobody complains that bastions or XP is too expensive despite requiring so many materials. Nobody complains that to get a colored name you need to donate.
Page 23 of 25
Why should cosmetic materials be treated differently than utilitarian ones? In sum, if you like trading, PvP, politics, exploring, or getting to know other people, you are for resource scarcity.

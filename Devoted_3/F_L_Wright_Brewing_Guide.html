Brewing is one of the most rewarding pastimes in this world. The resulting beverages can have varying effects, ranging from short bonus powers to intoxication modifications, and even blackout.

Cauldron A cauldron is easily constructed from 7 iron ingots. The block below must be empty and lit with flint & steel. A bucket of water fills it and 3 bottles empty it.
Page 6 of 49
Brewing Stand It can be tough to get blaze rods for a brewing stand. Trade or take snowballs to hunt rare blazes. Glowstone dust in the top slot temporarily turns it into a still without consuming the glowstone dust.
Page 7 of 49
Barrels They can be made from Spruce, Birch, Oak, Dark Oak, Acacia, and Jungle Wood. 8 stairs and a sign with BARREL in the first line make a small barrel. Console will confirm creating a barrel. ...
Page 8 of 49
... A large barrel is made from 16 stairs, 18 planks, 5 fences, and a sign. The small barrel has the sign in the lower right front, the large barrel in the top center front.
Page 9 of 49
Bottles Sand in a furnace makes glass and 3 glass make 3 bottles. You will need more than that. Also good for storing experience at an enchanting table before getting drunk!
Page 10 of 49
Bucket Brewers only need one, but they need it all the time. It gets water into cauldrons. It gets milk from a cow. And when a rum party got firy, it douses.
Page 11 of 49
Water Source Right next to the cauldrons. Make a 2x2 hole and fill the top left and lower right (or vice versa) corners with a source block from a bucket. Don't fall in when drunk. Embarrasing...
Page 12 of 49
Clock Made from 4 gold ingots surrounding 1 redstone dust, this usually frivolous tool is essential for brewing. Touch it to a cauldron and the current fermentation time is shown.
Page 13 of 49
Cow A cow is a good friend to have during long hours of work in the brewery. Its gentle mooing will become your mantra. And enough milk cures any poisoning, nausea, and slurred chat.

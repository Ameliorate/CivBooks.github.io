THE BUILDING ACT The Building Act gives every citizen the right to have a house, as long as it does not exceed 30 x 30 blocks. Things such as flower gardens that are outside the house are still considered part of the house, and therefore are

THE SNITCH ACT The Snitch Act defines where, when, and why citizens can place snitches, as well as the punishments for breaking these rules. In general, snitches must be well-hidden, reienforced to a Vogelburg Group, and have a meaningful
Page 13 of 21
name. If someone wants to put a private snitch in their house, they must ask an official for permission to do so. 'An official' is defined as a member of the Council of Ministers or someone the Council of Ministers appoints for that specific purpose.

THE CRIMES ACT All offences are categorized into three types: Crimes, Misdemeanours, and Simple Offences. There are also three differant scales for punishment, where Scale 1 is the worst and Scale 3 is not nearly as bad. If the offence is a Crime,
Page 15 of 21
your punishment could be one of the Scale 1 punishments. Misdemeanours give punishments on Scale 2, and Simple Offences give punishment on Scale 3. Scale 1 punishments include Exile and Imprisonment, while Scale 2 includes Exile and Imprisonment,

but for less time, as well as Hard Labor. Scale 3's punishments include Imprisonment and Hard Labor. Some examples of offences that range the Scales are murder, sharing secrets that jeopardize Vogelburg's or another nation's safety, urging someone else to
